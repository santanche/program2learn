# Clinical Case

A 42-year-old patient presents to the emergency department reporting a sudden onset of intense chest discomfort that began about an hour ago while resting. The pain is described as a heavy pressure in the center of the chest, radiating toward the left arm. The patient also reports difficulty breathing, which has progressively worsened since the onset of the pain.

In addition, the patient feels nauseated and appears visibly anxious. There is mild sweating, and the patient reports a sense of impending doom. No recent history of respiratory infection, fever, or cough is noted.

On examination, the patient is pale, with slightly elevated heart rate and respiratory rate. Oxygen saturation is mildly reduced. The clinical presentation raises immediate concern and warrants urgent investigation and intervention.
